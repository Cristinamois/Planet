Overview

Neptune, the furthest major planet from the Sun, is an ice giant with a deep blue atmosphere composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and methane. It is known for its violent winds and large dark spot. Neptune also has rings and moons, with Triton orbiting in the opposite direction to the planet's rotation.
