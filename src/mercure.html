Overview

Mercury, the closest planet to the Sun, is a rocky sphere pockmarked with craters resulting from meteorite impacts. Its hostile environment is characterized by extreme temperatures, with scorching heat during the day and freezing cold at night. Its surface is marked by plains, mountains, and steep cliffs.
